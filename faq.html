Frequently Asked Questions.

Why can't I just use a Tailwind plugin for dark mode?
Plugins are great for prototypes, but enterprise SaaS requires semantic token mapping. A generic plugin might invert a warning button to a color that no longer looks like a warning, or create text-on-background combinations that fail WCAG contrast requirements. We design perceptual dark modes that maintain brand identity and pass accessibility standards mathematically.
What is WCAG AA vs AAA?
The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) have levels of conformance. Level AA is the standard required by most accessibility laws (like the ADA in the US or EAA in Europe). It requires a contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text. Level AAA is the gold standard, requiring 7.1:1 contrast and stricter structural rules.
Do you provide code or just design?
We provide both. We map out the design system in Figma using proper accessibility plugins, and then our engineering team translates that into a robust CSS Custom Property (Variable) architecture, Tailwind configs, or SCSS files that you can drop directly into your React/Vue/Angular codebase.
